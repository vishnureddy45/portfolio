Letter Fulfillment via Hyperledger Fabric

Multi-OrgBlockchain Network
Real-TimeReconciliation
EliminatedManual Delays
ReducedFraud Risk

Built a multi-organisation blockchain network using Hyperledger Fabric to enable secure, real-time letter fulfillment and reconciliation across financial institutions — replacing a manual, error-prone process with a tamper-proof distributed ledger that all parties could trust.

Designed the shared ledger architecture with appropriate channel configurations to ensure each participating organisation had visibility into the transactions relevant to them while maintaining data privacy between competing institutions — a critical requirement in the BFSI context.

Implemented smart contracts (chaincode) to encode the business rules governing letter fulfillment — automating the validation, approval and reconciliation steps that previously required manual intervention from multiple teams across different organisations.

The blockchain approach eliminated the reconciliation delays that plagued the legacy process: instead of batch reconciliation runs that took hours or days to identify discrepancies, the shared ledger provided instant, cryptographically verified confirmation that all parties were working from the same data.

Reduced fraud risk by making the fulfillment record immutable — any attempt to alter a letter fulfillment record after the fact was immediately detectable, providing a level of audit trail integrity that traditional database systems could not match.
